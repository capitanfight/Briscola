FAQ

  • How many cards are used to play Briscola?

    40 cards from a traditional Italian deck are used (suits: cups, swords, coins, clubs).

  • How many players can participate? Can you play with more than 5 people?

    Briscola is usually played by 2, 3, 4 (in teams), or 5 players. With more than 5, the classic rules aren’t followed.

  • How is the Briscola (trump suit) determined?

    After the cards are dealt, the next card revealed is the Briscola, which determines the dominant suit for the game.

  • How is the card score calculated?

    Card points are as follows:

    • Ace = 11 points
    • Three = 10 points
    • King = 4 points
    • Horse (Knight) = 3 points
    • Jack = 2 points
    • All other cards are worth 0 points

    The total for a full deck is 120 points.

  • Who wins the hand?

    The player who plays the highest card of the trump suit (Briscola) wins the hand, or, if no trump cards are played, the highest card of the suit that was led wins.

  • Are there variations of the game?

    Yes, there are several regional variants and custom rule versions.

  • What happens if two players play the same card?

    This isn't possible since each card is unique. If cards of equal value are played, the first one played wins.

  • Can the Briscola be changed during the game?

    No, the Briscola stays the same for the entire game.

  • What happens if a player runs out of cards?

    If the deck is exhausted, players continue playing with the cards in hand until the game ends.

  • Do you have to follow the suit of the first card played?

    No, unlike other trick-taking games, in Briscola you're not required to follow suit.

  • Can I play a Briscola even if it’s not required?

    Yes, you can play a Briscola card at any time.

  • How many cards are dealt to each player?

    It depends on the number of players: 2 players: 3 cards each 3 or more players: 3 cards each, the rest go in the draw pile.

  • What happens if a player plays the wrong card?

    In online games, the system usually prevents incorrect plays. In physical games, the move is corrected or canceled.

  • What happens if a player forgets to draw a card after winning a trick?

    In online games, the system draws automatically. In real-life games, the player loses the chance to draw a new card.

  • How is the starting player determined?

    Usually, the player to the right of the dealer goes first.
